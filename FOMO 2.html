FOMO

FOMO IS OFTEN TREATED AS A MODERN INVENTION, A SIDE EFFECT OF INSTAGRAM AND TIKTOK. BUT ITS ROOTS GO FAR DEEPER THAN ANY APP.

HUMAN BEINGS ARE FUNDAMENTALLY SOCIAL ANIMALS.
BEING PART OF A TRIBE MEANT ACCESS TO FOOT,
PROTECTION, AND REPRODUCTION.

BEING EXCLUDED FROM THE GROUP, LEFT BEHIND, REJECTED, FORGOTTEN, WAS NOT JUST UNPLEASANT.

[IT WAS POTENTIALLY FATAL]

THE BRAIN EVOLVED TO TREAT SOCIAL EXCLUSION
AS A GENUINE THREAT TO SURVIVAL,
ACTIVATING THE SAME ALARM SYSTEMS AS PHYSICAL DANGER.
THIS IS NOT A METAPHOR:
NEUROIMAGING STUDIES SHOW THAT SOCIAL REJECTION ACTIVATES
THE SAME NEURAL REGIONS AS PHYSICAL PAIN.

THIS ANCIENT WIRING HAS NOT DISAPPEARED. WHAT CHANGED IS THE ENVIRONMENT IT OPERATES IN.

BEYOND BRAIN CHEMISTRY, SEVERAL WELL-ESTABLISHED PSYCHOLOGICAL THEORIES EXPLAIN WHY FOMO IS SO PERSISTENT:

THEORY AUTHOR HOW IT EXPLAINS FOMO
[SOCIAL COMPARISON THEORY] LEON FESTINGER, 1954 HUMANS INSTINCTIVELY EVALUATE THEMSELVES BY COMPARING TO OTHERS — SOCIAL MEDIA MAKES THIS COMPARISON CONSTANT AND ASYMMETRIC
[SELF-DETERMINATION THEORY] DECI & RYAN, 1985 PEOPLE HAVE A FUNDAMENTAL NEED FOR RELATEDNESS — FOMO IS THE ANXIETY THAT THIS NEED IS GOING UNMET
[BELONGINGNESS HYPOTHESIS] BAUMEISTER & LEARY, 1995 THE NEED TO BELONG IS AS FUNDAMENTAL AS HUNGER — EXCLUSION FROM SOCIAL EVENTS ACTIVATES A DEPRIVATION RESPONSE
[CONTINGENT SELF-WORTH] CROCKER & WOLFE, 2001 WHEN SELF-ESTEEM DEPENDS ON EXTERNAL SOCIAL SIGNALS, ANY PERCEIVED EXCLUSION BECOMES A DIRECT THREAT TO IDENTITY
