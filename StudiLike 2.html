WHEN PEOPLE KNOW THEIR CONTENT WILL BE PUBLICLY SCORED,

[THEY PERFORM]

RESEARCH ON THIS PHENOMENON SHOWS SEVERAL CONSISTENT PATTERNS:

BEHAVIOR WHAT DRIVES IT
[DELETING POSTS THAT UNDERPERFORM] THE NUMBER OF LIKES FUNCTIONS AS A PUBLIC VERDICT, A LOW SCORE FEELS LIKE PUBLIC HUMILIATION
[TIMING POSTS STRATEGICALLY] USERS LEARN WHEN THEIR AUDIENCE IS MOST ACTIVE TO MAXIMISE ENGAGEMENT, TREATING EACH POST AS A PERFORMANCE TO BE OPTIMISED
[COMPARING METRICS TO OTHERS COSTANTLY] FOLLOWER COUNTS CREATE AN EXPLICIT LEADERBOARD, ONE THAT IS IMPOSSIBLE TO IGNORE WHEN IT IS ALWAYS VISIBLE
[CHANGING BEHAVIOUR TO ATTRACT FOLLOWERS] THE DESIRE TO RAISE ONE'S 'SCORE' CAN LEAD PEOPLE TO ABANDON AUTHENTIC EXPRESSION IN FAVOUR OF WHAT THE ALGORITHM REWARDS

THE LIKE AND FOLLOWER COUNT DO NOT STAY INSIDE THE APP. THEY LEAK INTO THE REAL WORLD IN WAYS THAT ARE INCREASINGLY DOCUMENTED:

DOMAIN HOW METRICS CREATE REAL-WORLD CONSEQUENCES
[EMPLOYMENT] RECRUITERS AND EMPLOYERS ROUTINELY CHECK SOCIAL MEDIA, A LOW FOLLOWER COUNT OR SPARSE ENGAGEMENT CAN COST SOMEONE AN OPPORTUNITY
[ROMANTIC RELATIONSHIPS] STUDIES SHOW THAT FOLLOWER COUNT INFLUENCES PERCEIVED ATTRACTIVENESS AND SOCIAL DESIRABILITY ON DATING APPS AND PLATFORMS
[MENTAL HEALTH] ADOLESCENTS WITH LOW ENGAGEMENT METRICS REPORT SIGNIFICANTLY HIGHER RATES OF ANXIETY, DEPRESSION, AND FEELINGS OF WORTHLESSNESS
[SELF-PERCEPTION] OVER TIME, INDIVIDUALS BEGIN TO INTERNALIZE THE SCORE, EQUATING THEIR NUMBER OF FOLLOWERS WITH THEIR ACTUAL SOCIAL VALUE AS A HUMAN BEING
[POLITICAL INFLUENCE] POLITICIANS AND PUBLIC FIGURES WITH HIGH ENGAGEMENT ARE PERCEIVED AS MORE CREDIBLE AND REPRESENTATIVE OF PUBLIC OPINION
[CREATIVITY] THE PRESSURE TO PRODUCE CONTENT THAT WILL PERFORM ALGORITHMICALLY DISCOURAGES RISK-TAKING AND ORIGINALITY, THE MOST AUTHENTIC CREATIVE WORK RARELY GOES VIRAL
[EVALUATING THE CREDIBILITY OF INFORMATION] HIGH-METRIC ACCOUNTS ARE PERCEIVED AS MORE TRUSTWORTHY, EVEN WHEN THE INFORMATION IS FALSE OR UNVERIFIED
[INEQUALITY] THE SCORING SYSTEM ADVANTAGES THOSE WHO ALREADY HAVE SOCIAL CAPITAL, MAKING IT HARDER FOR NEW VOICES TO BE HEARD AND REINFORCING EXISTING HIERARCHIES RATHER THAN DEMOCRATISING EXPRESSION
