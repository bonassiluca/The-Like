FNE

FNE [FEAR OF NEGATIVE EVALUATION] IS DEFINED AS A "SENSE OF DREAD ASSOCIATED WITH BEING NEGATIVELY JUDGED IN A SOCIAL SITUATION."
ONLINE, THAT SOCIAL SITUATION IS PERMANENT.
THE JUDGMENT NEVER STOPS.

TO UNDERSTAND FNE, WE NEED TO UNDERSTAND HOW THE BRAIN PROCESSES SOCIAL STATUS:

TYPE OF EVALUATION EFFECT ON SOCIAL STATUS BRAIN RESPONSE
NEGATIVE EVALUATION [NE] DOWNWARD SHIFT, LOSS OF STANDING THREAT / STRESS RESPONSE
POSITIVE EVALUATION [PE] UPWARD SHIFT, GAIN OF STANDING REWARD / RELIEF RESPONSE

A LANDMARK STUDY INVESTIGATED
HOW FNE AFFECTS TWO KEY COMPONENTS OF SOCIAL BEHAVIOUR:
ANTICIPATION OF FEEDBACK AND PROCESSING OF FEEDBACK.

PARTICIPANTS WERE SPLIT INTO A HIGH FNE GROUP AND A LOW FNE GROUP.
EACH WAS SHOWN A PHOTO OF A STRANGER AND ASKED:

WOULD THIS PERSON ACCEPT OR REJECT ME?

THEY THEN RECEIVED FEEDBACK.
BRAIN ACTIVITY WAS MEASURED THROUGHOUT USING EEG.

EEG MEASURE WHAT IT CAPTURES HIGH FNE GROUP RESULTS
STIMULUS-PRECEDING NEGATIVITY [SPN] BRAIN ACTIVITY BEFORE RECEIVING FEEDBACK SIGNIFICANTLY HIGHER, MORE ANXIETY IN ANTICIPATION
FEEDBACK-RELATED NEGATIVITY [FRN] BRAIN'S IMMEDIATE REACTION TO THE OUTCOME ELEVATED RESPONSE TO NEGATIVE RESULTS
P300 WAVE [P3] DEPTH OF ATTENTION GIVEN TO FEEDBACK LARGER RESPONSE AFTER POSITIVE FEEDBACK, THEY NEEDED IT MORE
